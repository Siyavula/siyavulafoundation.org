Working at Siyavula

We’re looking for people that are enthusiastic about challenging problems and making a real impact in the world. Join us as we continue to build out world-class Intelligent Practice, our machine-learning driven, adaptive practice service.

We firmly believe fixing education, both in South Africa and further afield, is instrumental to bringing about real change. To that end, we aim to find ways for machines to do what machines do best with the hope of enabling people to, in turn, do their best.

Our dynamic and highly driven team work out of offices in the centre of incredible Cape Town, South Africa. We work hard and thrive off finding out what other companies in the technology and education sectors are doing and making sure our innovations keep us at the forefront.

We’re proud geeks and no, that does not mean we all have backgrounds in the hard-core Sciences! We get a kick out of working with each other, leveraging our powerful pool of diverse skills to collaboratively make a difference to education. We hold ourselves and each other to a very high standard of work. We appreciate people who are good at coming alongside and working together on a project, but are also capable of managing their own efforts, getting on a with the job at hand and delivering a high-quality product, every time.

As part of our team, you’d get to work on a wide variety of projects, so there’s always lots to learn and plenty of opportunity to really get stuck in.

Sound like your kind of work environment? We may need you.

Front-end Developer

Role and Responsibilities

In the context of our online educational offerings, you would be expected to:

  • help the team in designing and developing new features, including but not restricted to interactive visualisations, simulations, client-side book and exercise renderers and dashboards;
  • implementing the necessary HTML, CSS and JS to make new features work across browsers and platforms; and
  • ensuring features and elements work well and load fast.

The kind of work you could be part of

  • redesigning our learner experience, both for responsive and feature-phone friendly interfaces;
  • developing new interfaces for educator dashboards, assignment setting and reporting;
  • analysing the data collected to provide better feedback to learners and teachers;
  • aggregating and visualising data to inform policy-makers and curriculum-design; and
  • visualisation of concept maps to inform teaching practice.

Requirements

If you’re the candidate we’re looking for you have a keen self-awareness and ability to determine opportunities for your own growth; enthusiasm for working in a team; and passion regarding your own continued learning. Most of all, you should want to make a difference to education in South Africa and around the world.

To fill this role you should also ideally have past experience that includes:

  • using openly-licensed software and libraries, git and GitHub;
  • interpreting graphic, visual or interaction designs in HTML, CSS and JS;
  • creating effective web pages or interfaces;
  • creating, editing or modifying templates for a CMS or web development framework;
  • experience using a framework for interactions like JQuery, YUI or Prototype;
  • testing cross-browser, cross-platform, and/or cross-device compatibility for inconsistencies; and
  • testing for compliance to web standards.

Experience developing visualisations using a framework like D3 or simulations in HTML5/CSS3/JS would be extremely advantageous.

You should be familiar with the technology stack we use (or keen to learn and quick to upskill) and have at least 4 years experience under your belt.

Salary

In line with experience and market related.

Back-end Developer

Role and Responsibilities

In the context of our online educational offerings, you would be expected to:

  • work with the team to design and develop offerings;
  • extend and develop SaaS applications that optimise the learning experience for individual and school learners;
  • develop applications for reporting, monitoring and evaluation of applications and their impact; and
  • build tools and applications for data analysis.

The kind of work you could be part of

  • deploying, managing and synchronising a fleet of servers to be placed at schools with very poor connectivity (some virtual and some physical);
  • scaling to 100 000s of active users;
  • developing an API to allow integration into other platforms;

Requirements

If you’re the candidate we’re looking for you have a keen self-awareness and ability to determine opportunities for your own growth; enthusiasm for working in a team; and passion regarding your own continued learning. Most of all, you should want to make a difference to education in South Africa and around the world.

To fill this role you should also ideally have past experience that includes:

  • custom web application development;
  • development with at least one CMS framework or web application framework;
  • employing a framework or extendeding an existing CMS with custom coding;
  • a working knowledge of security practices;
  • an understanding of the underlying systems on which web applications are developed; and
  • an understanding of and experience applying key server-side web development concepts such as:
    • integrating data or user accounts between multiple systems for a client;
    • integrating multiple databases and/or database types into one system;
    • scaling an application and/or its hosting environment to support increases in traffic;
    • using a version control system;
    • implementing or working with content approval and staging workflows;
    • migrating data, scripting migration of data;
    • implementing or working with a template driven system with a clear separation between content and display;
    • outputting data in multiple formats;
    • experience on a web application that uses XML HTTP Request and JS;
    • working with multiple delivery platforms such as mobile versus desktop;
    • creating multilingual websites or applications;
    • creating and implementing database schemas that represent and support business processes; and
    • ensuring the quality of applications through testing frameworks.

Experience developing with Python as a language and Pyramid as a framework would be extremely advantageous.

You should be familiar with the technology stack we use (or keen to learn and quick to upskill) and have at least 4 years experience under your belt.

Salary

In line with experience and market related.
